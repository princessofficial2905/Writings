The Day I Realized

I Deserved More.

February 2026
Read

There comes a moment, quiet, subtle, almost sacred, when something in your heart shifts. It doesn't happen through a dramatic heartbreak or a loud goodbye. It happens in the stillness. In the middle of an ordinary day, during an ordinary thought, when suddenly a truth rises inside you like a soft sunrise: "I deserve more than this."

More than half-love. More than mixed signals. More than being chosen only when it's convenient. More than staying loyal to someone who barely shows up. More than explaining your worth to someone who never learned how to see it. That single thought, that gentle awakening, becomes the beginning of a new life you didn't even know was waiting for you.

Breadcrumbs are not a meal. Fragments are not a future. Effort once a month is not love.

For so long, you accepted less not because you didn't deserve better, but because you hadn't yet realized how valuable your heart truly is. You told yourself to be patient. You told yourself the bare minimum was enough. You convinced yourself that breadcrumbs were a meal, that fragments were a future, that effort once a month counted as love. You waited for change that never came. You justified behaviors that hurt your spirit. You stayed in places where your heart was starving. And still, a part of you whispered, "It's okay… maybe this is just how love is."

But slowly, God began working in the background of your soul, softening the parts of you that were too afraid to want more, strengthening the parts of you that were too tired to keep settling. And one day, the veil lifted. You saw your worth clearly. You saw the truth of your heart. You saw the amount of love you carried, patient, deep, tender, healing, and you realized how wrong it was that you were giving it to someone who didn't know how to treat it as sacred.

That realization wasn't arrogance. It wasn't pride. It wasn't selfishness. It was alignment. It was clarity. It was divine intervention.
God wasn't trying to take love away from you, He was trying to lead you toward the love you've always prayed for. The love that won't make you question yourself. The love that won't leave you waiting. The love that won't confuse your heart or drain your spirit. The love that stays, chooses, commits, and cherishes.

One day soon, my friend, you will look back and marvel at the moment you finally recognized your own value. Because that moment, the day you realized you deserved more, was the exact day your destiny started shifting. It was the day God began opening doors to relationships, opportunities, and blessings that match the depth of your soul. You didn't lose anything by letting go of less. You gained the entire future you were created for.

The day you realized you deserved more
was the day God whispered,

"Now you're ready for what I've been saving for you."
P
