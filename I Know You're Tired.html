I Know
You're Tired.

by Princess  ·  February 2026  ·  4 min

The Essay

I know you're tired. Not the kind of tired that sleep can fix. Not the kind that goes away after a good night's rest or a lazy Sunday morning.

You're tired in your soul. In that deep place where no one else can see. The place where you keep all the things you don't say out loud.

You're tired of pretending. Tired of putting on that smile when someone asks "How are you?" and answering "I'm good" when everything inside you wants to scream "I'm not okay. I haven't been okay for a long time."

You're tired of being strong. Of being the one everyone leans on. The one who always has it together. The one people call when they're falling apart, never realizing that you're barely holding yourself together.

You're tired of trying. Trying to be better, do better, feel better. Trying to fix what feels permanently broken inside you. Trying to keep up with a world that never stops moving while you feel like you're standing still.

I know you're tired of caring so much. About people who don't care back. About situations you can't control. About outcomes you can't change no matter how hard you try.

You're tired of the same thoughts spinning in your head at 2 AM. The same regrets. The same what-ifs. The same painful memories that play on repeat like a song you can't turn off.

You're tired of starting over. Of picking up the pieces again. Of rebuilding what fell apart. Of hoping again when hope has disappointed you so many times before.

You're tired of being disappointed. By people you trusted. By opportunities that didn't work out. By yourself, for not being who you thought you'd be by now.

You're tired of explaining yourself. Of justifying your feelings. Of defending your choices. Of making yourself smaller so others feel comfortable.

You're tired of waiting. For things to get better. For people to change. For your life to finally feel like it's yours. For that moment when everything clicks into place and makes sense.

I know you're tired of fighting. Fighting to be seen. Fighting to be heard. Fighting to be understood. Fighting battles no one knows you're fighting because you've learned to bleed quietly.

You're tired of being alone, even when you're surrounded by people. That lonely that comes from being misunderstood, from hiding parts of yourself, from knowing that no one really knows the real you, the scared you, the broken you, the you that cries in the shower so no one hears.

You're tired of your own mind. The overthinking. The anxiety. The voice that tells you you're not enough. The memories that won't fade. The fears that won't quiet down.

And today, you're just tired. So tired that even getting out of bed felt like climbing a mountain. So tired that simple tasks feel impossible. So tired that you wonder how much longer you can keep doing this.

I see you. I see how hard you're trying. I see the effort you put into just existing some days. I see the strength it takes to keep going when everything in you wants to stop.

And I want to tell you something: It's okay to be tired.

It's okay to not have energy for anything today. It's okay to cancel plans. It's okay to say no. It's okay to choose yourself for once. It's okay to rest without feeling guilty about it.

You don't have to earn the right to be tired. You don't have to justify it. You don't have to prove to anyone that you're struggling enough to deserve a break.

You're tired because you've been carrying too much for too long. And that's not weakness. That's being human.

So please, rest. Not just your body, but your mind. Your heart. Your soul.

Stop pushing yourself to be productive. Stop forcing yourself to be positive. Stop pretending you're fine when you're not.

Just rest.

The world will still be here tomorrow. The problems will still be there. The responsibilities won't disappear. But right now, in this moment, you can just be. Tired. Real. Human.

You don't have to fight today. You don't have to be strong. You don't have to do anything except breathe and exist.

And tomorrow? Tomorrow you can try again. Or not. You can rest again if you need to. There's no timeline for healing. No deadline for feeling better.

You're doing better than you think you are. Even on days when all you did was survive. Especially on those days.

So rest now, tired soul. You've earned it a thousand times over.

And when you're ready, not when the world tells you to be ready, but when you actually are, you'll find your strength again.

But today? Today it's okay to just be tired.

You're still here. Still trying. Still surviving.

And that's more than enough.
You are more than enough. Even when you're tired. Especially when you're tired.

With understanding,
Someone who knows that tired feeling too — Princess
