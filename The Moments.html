The Moments

I Pretended Not to Care.

Princess · February 2026 · 2 min read

There comes a moment when you finally face the truth you hid even from yourself, you pretended not to care, not because you didn't feel anything, but because you felt too much.

You acted unbothered when your heart was aching. You stayed silent when you wanted to scream. You shrugged things off because you didn't want to seem sensitive. You told yourself, "It's fine," even when your spirit was cracking. You played it cool because you were afraid that showing how deeply you cared would make you look weak, needy, or easy to hurt. But pretending not to care never protected you… it only isolated your heart further.

There were moments you acted like their inconsistency didn't affect you,

moments you laughed off their distance,

moments you pretended their mixed signals didn't confuse you,

moments you tried to look unaffected when, inside, you were unraveling.

You hid your disappointment behind jokes.

You hid your longing behind busyness.

You hid your heartbreak behind a calm face you mastered so well.

And though the world couldn't see it, God always could.

He saw the way you held tears behind your eyes so no one would think you were fragile. He saw the way you kept choosing strength when all you really wanted was honesty and love. He saw the way you minimized your emotions just to keep someone close, the way you convinced yourself that your feelings were "too much," the way you hid your heart so nobody could mishandle it again. And gently, He whispers:

"Your emotions were never the problem, the people you gave them to were."

Pretending not to care didn't make you stronger. It only taught you how much of yourself you were willing to hide to feel safe. But you're healing now. And healing means learning to honor your emotions instead of burying them. Healing means embracing your softness instead of apologizing for it. Healing means acknowledging the things that hurt, the things that made you anxious, the things that disappointed you, and giving your heart permission to feel without shame.

✦

One day, My friend, you will meet someone who makes you feel safe enough to stop pretending.

Someone who listens when you speak,

who notices when something is wrong,

who asks instead of assuming,

who stays instead of pulling away.

And with them, caring will not feel like a risk,

it will feel like a home.

Your feelings will be met, not ignored.

Your emotions will be honored, not judged.

Your heart will be held, not dismissed.

The moments you pretended not to care were the moments you protected yourself the only way you knew how. But soon, you'll discover a love where you won't ever have to pretend again.
