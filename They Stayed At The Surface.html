An Essay by Princess

They Stayed at
the Surface

and Blamed Me for My Depth

February 2026 · 3 min read
✦

There comes a moment when you finally face the truth you tried to avoid for so long, the version of you someone chose to see was never the whole you. They loved the parts that were easy, the parts that fit their comfort, the parts that demanded nothing. But the deeper parts of you, the fears, the softness, the insecurities, the dreams, the tenderness, the unspoken needs, they never took the time to understand.

They liked the idea of you, not the reality of you. And for a long time, you blamed yourself, wondering if maybe you were too complicated, too emotional, too much. But now, with clearer eyes, you see the truth: you were never too much, they were simply too unwilling.

There were layers to you they never explored. They didn't ask why your heart cared the way it did. They didn't ask what shaped your sensitivity. They didn't ask what made you afraid, what made you hopeful, what made you strong. They didn't ask what kept you awake at night or what made you feel safe. You were ready to give them access to your soul, but they stayed at the surface, and then blamed you for the depth they never tried to understand.

And still, My friend, you tried. You explained, you opened up, you softened, you made yourself easier for them to love. You handed them pieces of your heart gently, hoping they would finally see you, really see you. But some people don't have the emotional capacity to understand someone with depth. They only understand simplicity. So they chose the version of you that took the least effort to love, the least responsibility to hold, the least courage to meet.

You were never hard to understand. You were simply offering yourself to someone who never intended to learn you.

But God saw the fullness of you. He saw the layers of your heart they never reached. He saw the tenderness you offered, the honesty you tried to share, the vulnerability you risked giving. And He knew you deserved someone who would meet your soul with reverence, not avoidance. Someone who sees your depth and doesn't run. Someone who looks into your heart and says, "I want to understand everything about you." Someone who chooses the real you, not the convenient version.

One day, My friend, you'll meet someone who studies you the way others never did. Someone who pays attention. Someone who remembers the details. Someone who listens not to respond, but to understand. Someone who asks questions because your mind fascinates them, your heart matters to them, your emotions are safe with them. And in that moment, you'll realize you were never hard to understand, you were simply offering yourself to someone who never intended to learn you.

The version of you they ignored will be the version someone else treasures.   And when that love arrives, you'll finally feel what it's like to be seen all the way through.
