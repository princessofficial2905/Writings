The Love
That Wasn't Meant
for Me

on heartbreak, clarity & divine protection

February 2026
3 min read
Princess
Read

There comes a moment when you finally recognize that not every love you wanted was meant to stay, and not every person you held onto so tightly was meant to walk with you into the future. It's a painful truth, especially when your heart was ready, ready to grow, ready to commit, ready to give in ways you never had before.

You saw something in them that felt like destiny, like alignment, like possibility. You believed the connection was special because, to you, it truly was. But sometimes the love you felt wasn't mutual in depth, or timing, or readiness. Sometimes you were prepared for a chapter they were never meant to write with you. And understanding that doesn't dull the ache, but it does bring clarity.

✦
You tried to make sense of it. You wondered what you lacked. You replayed moments hoping to find the reason. You questioned whether you were too much or not enough.

But the more you search for explanations, the more you realize something you had been avoiding: the love that wasn't meant for you will always feel fragile, inconsistent, confusing, and heavy. It will ask you to prove your worth. It will make you shrink so they feel comfortable. It will make you chase instead of rest. It will demand more pieces of you than it ever gives in return.

Love that isn't meant for you never feels safe. It feels like uncertainty dressed up as hope.

✦
But God has a way of protecting you even through heartbreak. He saw the nights you wished things were different. He saw the softness you poured into someone who couldn't receive it. He saw the effort you gave that went unappreciated. He saw the parts of yourself you sacrificed trying to make it work.

And instead of letting you stay in a love that would have slowly drained you, He stepped in, not cruelly, but compassionately, and allowed it to slip away. Because the love you thought you wanted was too small for the life He's preparing you for. It wasn't rejection; it was protection. It was God saying, "Not this. Not them. I have better for you."

✦
One day, My friend, you will meet a love that feels entirely different. A love that stays. A love that chooses you clearly and consistently. A love that feels like peace instead of guessing. A love that doesn't make you abandon yourself just to keep it.

With this person, you won't wonder if you're enough, you'll know you are. You won't chase, you'll be met. You won't question their heart, you'll feel it in every action. And in that moment, you'll understand why the love you once wanted so badly was never meant to be yours.

The love that wasn't meant for you didn't fail.

It freed you.

It opened space in your heart for a love that is deeper, truer, and God-ordained.


And what's meant for you, My friend,

will arrive with certainty,

and stay with grace.

end of essay
