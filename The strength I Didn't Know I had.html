The Strength
I Didn't Know
I Had

Until I Needed It.

February 2026
2 min read
Reflection
Read
✦ ✦ ✦

There comes a moment in your life when you finally realize that the strength you always admired in others had been quietly living inside you the whole time. You never saw it, because strength doesn't show up when everything is fine, it appears when your world starts shaking, when the people you trusted walk away, when dreams fall apart, when life tests you in ways you never prepared for. And in those moments, you don't rise with confidence. You rise with trembling hands, tear-filled eyes, and a heart that whispers, "I don't know how, but I'll keep going."

That whisper, my friend, that is strength.

For so long you believed you were fragile. Too soft. Too emotional. Too easily wounded. But look at everything you survived without even realizing how powerful you were becoming. You carried heartbreaks that could've hardened you, but you stayed gentle. You faced losses that could've destroyed you, but you kept believing. You walked through seasons that could've drowned you, but somehow you still rose. Strength didn't arrive in a moment, it grew inside you slowly, quietly, faithfully.

God watched every step you took in the dark. He saw the nights you cried yourself to sleep and still woke up trying. He saw the days you felt completely empty but still gave love to others. He saw the battles you fought silently because you didn't want to worry anyone. And He wants you to know something tender: the strength that carried you wasn't accidental, it was divine. He placed that resilience within you long before you ever needed it.

One day, you will look back at the version of yourself who walked through storms barefoot and bleeding, and you will feel nothing but admiration for her.

She didn't know she was strong, she simply refused to give up. She didn't know she was capable, she simply kept trying. She didn't know she was growing, she simply survived, one breath at a time.

And here's the most beautiful part: the strength you have now is not the strength you started with, it's the strength life built in you, the strength God awakened in you, the strength you earned by standing back up every time something tried to break you.

My friend, you are stronger than you think,
softer than you admit,
braver than you feel,
and more protected than you know.

And the next chapter of your life will not require
the strength of survival,
it will require the strength to receive
blessings, love, peace, and joy without fear.
