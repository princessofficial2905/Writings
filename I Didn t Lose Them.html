Lose Them.

Somewhere Along the Way, I Lost Me.
February 2026

There comes a moment in your healing when you suddenly remember the promises you once made to yourself, the ones whispered late at night when your heart was tired, the ones formed after heartbreak, the ones you crafted in moments of clarity and strength. Promises like, "I will never settle again," "I will protect my peace," "I won't let anyone make me feel small," "I will choose myself first." You meant every word… but somewhere along the way, life got heavy, love got confusing, loneliness got loud, and you forgot the vows you made to your own heart. Not intentionally. Not carelessly. But because you were trying so hard to hold on to people and moments that didn't know how to hold you back.

✦

You broke your own promises in ways you would never break them for anyone else.

You let things slide that should've been boundaries.

You tolerated behaviors you swore you never would.

You shrank your worth to keep connections that were already slipping.

You silenced your needs because you didn't want to lose someone who wasn't choosing you fully.

And the saddest part isn't that you forgot the promises, it's that you forgot yourself in the process.

✦

But here is the gentle grace of it all: God never forgot. Not the woman you wanted to become. Not the strength you desired to step into. Not the peace you longed for. Not the standards you set when your heart was clear. He remembered every promise you whispered in pain, in hope, in fear, in determination. And when you wandered away from them, He didn't punish you, He redirected you. He let certain relationships fall apart so you could return to yourself. He removed certain people so your heart could finally breathe. He closed certain doors because He knew you deserved more than what you were settling for.

One day, My friend, you will finally feel those forgotten promises rise within you again.

You will wake up and remember the woman you said you would become. You will feel your boundaries strengthening, your worth settling into your bones, your heart becoming whole again. And you will keep those promises not out of fear or heartbreak this time, but out of love, love for yourself, love for your future, love for the version of you God is shaping with tenderness and grace.

✦

You didn't fail yourself.
You simply wandered.
And now, you are finding your way back,
to your voice,
to your standards,
to your heart,
to the promises that were always meant to guide you toward the life and love you deserve.
