A Personal Essay

The Weight
Wasn't the
Problem

The Silence Was.

scroll

There comes a moment in your life when you finally feel the weight of everything you kept inside, the emotions you swallowed, the truths you silenced, the tears you blinked back, the questions you never asked, the pain you never admitted, the longing you hid behind strength. You didn't hold it all in because you wanted to suffer. You held it in because you didn't feel safe enough to let it out. Because you didn't want to be a burden. Because you didn't want to seem dramatic. Because you didn't want to lose people by showing how deeply you feel. And so, piece by piece, year by year, moment by moment, you carried emotional weight no one ever knew about, and it changed you.

✦
You got used to being the strong one, even when strength felt like a costume you put on every morning.
You got used to saying "I'm okay," even when your chest felt tight.
You got used to comforting others while secretly praying for someone to comfort you.
You got used to being the listener because no one ever asked you how you were really doing.
You held so much inside that the silence began to feel safer than vulnerability.
But deep down, you knew, your heart wasn't meant to hold this much alone.

your heart wasn't meant to hold this much alone.

And God? He saw everything you hid from the world.
He saw the nights you cried quietly into your pillow.
He heard the prayers you whispered with a shaky voice.
He watched you carry burdens that were never meant to be carried alone.
And while you thought no one noticed your pain, God held every unspoken word, every suppressed emotion, every deep ache you tucked away.
He knew you weren't weak for holding it all in, you were scared, you were tired, you were trying.
And He never judged you for that.
✦

Slowly, without you realizing it, He began softening the tightness around your heart. He began creating space for healing you didn't even know you needed. He sent you moments that forced you to release what you'd been storing for years, through tears, through realizations, through endings, through prayers. It didn't all come out at once. But little by little, the emotional load you carried began to lift. And you started to breathe again, fully, honestly, freely.

One day, My friend, you will look back and wonder how you ever carried so much in silence. You will feel proud of the woman who survived emotional storms without letting them break her. You will be grateful for the version of yourself who held everything together even when she was falling apart inside. And most beautifully, you will learn to live in a way where you don't have to hold everything in anymore, where your emotions have space, where your voice matters, where your heart is understood, and where love feels safe instead of heavy.

What you held inside for too long is finally being released, not through force, but through God's gentle, healing love.
