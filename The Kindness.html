An Essay by Princess

The
Kindness

I Never Received Back

✦

February 22, 2026   ·   3 min read

Read

There comes a moment when you finally allow yourself to see the truth you kept avoiding: you were kind to people who didn't know how to hold that kindness. You gave softness to hearts that were too hardened to feel it. You offered understanding to people who didn't try to understand you. You showed patience to those who took it for granted. You poured warmth into places that stayed cold no matter how much love you gave. And for the longest time, you wondered why. Why couldn't they give you even a fraction of what you gave them? Why was returning your kindness such a difficult task? Why did loving you gently seem so impossible for them?

They didn't lack the ability to be kind, they lacked the willingness to be kind to you.

And that hurts in a way that's hard to explain. Because you weren't asking for much. Just a little effort. Just a little reciprocity. Just a little softness that matched your own. You weren't expecting perfection. You weren't expecting grand gestures. All you ever wanted was emotional fairness, the kind of kindness that makes love feel safe instead of lonely. But instead, you found yourself giving more than you ever received, stretching your heart in ways no one stretched for you, and carrying emotional weight they never thought to lift.

✦

Still, even in your disappointment, even in your hurt, you never stopped being kind.

And that is your beauty.
Your kindness was never conditional.
It wasn't something you turned on or off based on how others treated you.
It came from your soul, not from their behavior.

And even though it sometimes made you feel unappreciated, forgotten, or overlooked, your kindness was never wasted, because it revealed something sacred: your heart's character.

✦

God saw every moment you were gentle with someone who wasn't gentle with you. He saw the patience you showed when others responded with indifference. He saw the compassion you offered to people who didn't know how to reciprocate it. He saw how you loved with grace even when you weren't loved the same way. And He wants you to know: nothing you gave was in vain. Your kindness is a reflection of Him, and He will return it to you through people who are capable of receiving it.

✦

One day, My friend, you will meet someone who treats your kindness like treasure. Someone who notices the way you care. Someone who doesn't just appreciate your tenderness, they protect it. Someone who reflects back the gentleness you've been giving the world alone for so long. And when that day comes, you'll finally understand why your kindness was never too much, it was simply being poured into the wrong hearts.

You will not spend your life giving and never receiving.
You will not keep loving people who only take.
You will not remain in spaces where your kindness is treated as convenience.

God is sending you people whose hearts match yours, kind, soft, present, intentional. People who know how to give back what you so effortlessly give out.

Your kindness wasn't wasted, My friend.
It was planting seeds for the love you're about to receive.

Princess

Just a girl named Princess… dreaming of that one soul who stumbles upon her words and finally feels less alone.
