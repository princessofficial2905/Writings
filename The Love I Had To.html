The Love I Had to Teach Myself

A meditation on self  ·  3 min read
Read
There comes a moment when you look at your reflection, not the one in the mirror, but the one in your soul, and realize that the love you were waiting to receive from others was the love you needed to learn to give yourself. For so long, you searched for someone to speak life into you, someone to reassure you, someone to choose you, someone to stay, someone to hold the parts of you you couldn't hold alone. You wanted someone to understand your softness, honor your heart, see your depth, and love you in the way you always loved others. But life kept sending you people who couldn't love you in the way you needed. And in the emptiness they left behind, you slowly began learning the truth: If you don't love yourself in the way you deserve, you will keep accepting love that falls short of your soul.
You weren't wrong for wanting love. You weren't wrong for yearning for gentleness, consistency, affection, and safety. But the people you handed your heart to were not capable of giving you the fullness of love you carry within you. You kept giving pieces of yourself to people who didn't even know how to hold themselves together. You kept asking for understanding from people who never understood their own emotions. You kept desiring loyalty from hearts that didn't have loyalty to offer. And somewhere along the line, you blamed yourself for expecting too much, when in reality, your expectations were simply aligned with the love God intended for you.
∴ The Sacred Removal

So He did something sacred. He removed the people who could not love you properly. Not as punishment. Not as cruelty. But as divine protection. He allowed their absence to become the space where you finally learned to love yourself the way they never did.

You learned to comfort yourself when others walked away. You learned to trust yourself when others left you confused. You learned to validate your emotions when no one else cared to. You learned that your heart is worthy of affection even when no one is offering it.

∴ The Most Beautiful Part
The love you are teaching yourself now is preparing your heart for the love God is bringing to you later.
  • A love that won't make you question your worth.
  • A love that won't disappear when things get hard.
  • A love that won't take your softness for granted.
  • A love that won't expect you to carry the emotional weight alone.
  • A love that will match the gentleness you've learned to give yourself.

One day, My friend, you will look back and realize that the love you were forced to give yourself became the foundation for everything beautiful that is coming. You will attract a love that mirrors the way you finally learned to treat your own heart. You will no longer accept crumbs because you've learned to feed your soul with compassion. You will no longer settle for uncertainty because you've learned to give yourself peace. You will no longer chase love because you've learned that real love doesn't require chasing, it meets you where you are.

✦   ✦   ✦
The love you taught yourself is the love that will protect you, heal you, and prepare you for the love that is meant for you.
