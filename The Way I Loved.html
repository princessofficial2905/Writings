The Way
I Loved

Before I Learned Better.

By Princess
Read

There comes a moment when you look back at the way you used to love, the way you gave without limits, trusted without hesitation, forgave without being asked, and stayed long after your heart began to break, and instead of feeling ashamed, you finally feel tenderness for that version of yourself. You loved with innocence, purity, hope, and depth. You didn't calculate. You didn't hold back. You didn't question whether someone deserved your heart, you simply offered it, believing that love alone could fix, heal, transform, or hold anything. That wasn't foolish. That was beautiful. That was human. That was you before the world taught you to be careful.

But over time, through heartbreak and disappointment, you learned lessons you never asked for.

You learned that not everyone loves with the same sincerity.
You learned that effort matters more than words.
You learned that loyalty isn't something you can teach someone who doesn't value it.
You learned that love cannot thrive in places built on confusion.
And you learned that giving your all to the wrong people only leaves you with fragments of yourself you have to gather alone.
✦   ✦   ✦

And as painful as those lessons were, they didn't harden you, they refined you.

You didn't stop loving deeply; you just stopped loving carelessly.
You didn't stop trusting; you started trusting yourself more.
You didn't stop giving; you learned to give in ways that don't drain you.
You didn't stop believing in love; you simply raised your standards for the kind of love that deserves to stay.

He saw you placing your heart in hands that didn't know how to hold it. And He didn't let those experiences break you — He used them to shape you.

God saw the way you loved before you knew better.
He saw your purity, your softness, your hopefulness, your willingness to pour into others even when you were running on empty.
He saw you placing your heart in hands that didn't know how to hold it.
And He didn't let those experiences break you, He used them to shape you.
He let you love deeply because that is who you are.
He let you learn through pain because wisdom is born from truth.
And now, He is guiding you toward the kind of love your heart was always meant for, a love that matches your purity instead of taking advantage of it.

✦   ✦   ✦

One day, My friend, you will love again, but not the way you used to.

You will love with clarity instead of confusion.
You will love with boundaries instead of self-abandonment.
You will love with confidence instead of fear.
You will love with wisdom instead of desperation.
And the right person will look at you and thank God for every version of you that learned, healed, softened, and grew.

The way you loved before wasn't wrong, it was simply unprotected.
And now, you are learning to love yourself with the same intensity you once reserved for others.
That is what "loving better" truly means.

Princess

Just a girl named Princess — dreaming of that one soul who stumbles upon her words and finally feels less alone.
