An essay by Princess

Scrolling
to Avoid

Your Own Thoughts

6 min read  ·  personal essay  ·  2026

SCROLL

I.

You reach for your phone the second you feel uncomfortable. The second there's silence. The second you're alone. The second you start to feel something you don't want to feel or think something you don't want to think.

Your hand moves automatically. Like a reflex. Like breathing. Before you even realize you're doing it, the phone is in your hand and you're scrolling.

Instagram. TikTok. Twitter. Reddit. YouTube. News. Anything. Everything. Just something to fill the space. Something to keep your brain occupied. Something to keep you from being alone with yourself.

Because being alone with yourself is terrifying.

Being alone means feeling. It means thinking. It means confronting all the things you've been avoiding. All the questions you don't have answers to. All the pain you don't know how to process. All the decisions you don't want to make.

So you scroll. And scroll. And scroll.

You tell yourself it's just for a minute. Just a quick break. Just checking something. Just killing time. But an hour passes. Two hours. Three hours. You've seen everything. You've absorbed nothing. You've been entertained by none of it but you can't stop.

Because stopping means thinking. And thinking means feeling. And feeling means dealing with everything you've been running from.

✦

You're not really looking at anything. You're not really engaged. You're just moving your thumb. Watching images and words blur past. Consuming without digesting. Scrolling without seeing.

It's numbing. That's the point. It's the modern version of staring at a wall or watching TV static. It's the digital equivalent of dissociation.

You're present but not present. Awake but checked out. Conscious but avoiding consciousness.

And it works. Sort of. For a while. It keeps the thoughts at bay. It keeps the feelings manageable. It keeps you distracted from the voice in your head asking questions you don't want to answer.

What am I doing with my life?

Am I happy?

Why does everything feel so hard?

What am I running from?

Scroll. Scroll. Scroll. Make the questions go away.

But they don't go away. They just wait. They accumulate. They build up behind the wall of distraction you've built. And the longer you scroll, the higher the wall needs to be.

II.

You've forgotten what it feels like to just exist. To sit in silence. To be bored. To let your mind wander. To think without distracting yourself from thinking.

You've trained your brain to need constant stimulation. Constant input. Constant distraction. And now, when there's nothing to scroll, you feel anxious. Restless. Uncomfortable in your own skin.

You're addicted. Not to the content. But to the numbness. To the escape. To the ability to avoid yourself without technically avoiding anything.

You're still there. You're still conscious. But you're not present. You're somewhere else. Somewhere safe. Somewhere that doesn't require you to confront anything real.

And everyone's doing it. Everyone's scrolling. Everyone's avoiding. Everyone's numbing. So it doesn't even feel like a problem. It feels normal. Like this is just what people do now.

But it is a problem. Because you're disappearing. Not dramatically. Not all at once. But slowly. Quietly. One scroll at a time.

You're losing time. Hours of your life spent consuming content you don't care about. Time you'll never get back. Time that could have been spent creating, connecting, healing, living.

You're losing connection. To yourself, to others, to the present moment. You're in the same room as people you love but you're not there. You're somewhere in your phone. Somewhere else. Anywhere but here.

You're losing your ability to be present. To focus. To think deeply. To sit with discomfort. To feel fully. Your attention span is shrinking. Your tolerance for boredom is gone. Your capacity to just be is disappearing.

You're losing yourself. The thoughts you're avoiding are important thoughts.

And by scrolling them away, you're not solving anything. You're just postponing. You're just delaying. You're just building up a bigger storm that will eventually hit.

Because you can't avoid yourself forever. Eventually, you have to face the things you're running from. Eventually, you have to feel the feelings. Eventually, you have to think the thoughts.

And the longer you wait, the harder it becomes. The wall gets higher. The avoidance gets stronger. The disconnection gets deeper.

III.

So what do you do? How do you stop? How do you break the cycle when the urge to scroll is so strong and the discomfort of not scrolling is so intense?

You start small. You catch yourself. You notice when you reach for your phone. You ask yourself why.

What am I avoiding right now?

What feeling am I running from?

What thought am I trying to escape?

And then, just for a moment, you don't scroll. You sit with the discomfort. You let the thought come. You feel the feeling. Just for a moment.

It will be uncomfortable. Incredibly uncomfortable. Your brain will scream at you to pick up the phone. To scroll. To escape. To numb.

But you don't. You sit. You breathe. You stay with yourself.

And slowly, gradually, painfully, you learn that you can survive being alone with yourself. That your thoughts won't destroy you. That your feelings, as intense as they are, are bearable.

On the other side of the scrolling, on the other side of the avoidance, there's you. The real you. The one you've been running from.

That person deserves to be heard. Deserves to be felt. Deserves to exist fully instead of being constantly numbed and distracted and avoided.

✦

So put the phone down. Not forever. Not perfectly. Just sometimes. Just for moments. Just to practice being with yourself.

Sit in the discomfort. Feel the feelings. Think the thoughts. Ask the questions. Face the things you've been avoiding.

It's scary. It's hard. It's everything you've been running from.

But it's also real. It's alive. It's you.

The thoughts you're avoiding? They're not as scary as you think.

The feelings you're numbing? They're trying to tell you something important.

The discomfort you're running from? That's where the growth is.

The scroll will always be there.
The distraction will always be available.
The escape will always be one swipe away.

But you? You're here now.
Present. Alive. Real.

Stop scrolling.
Start living.
