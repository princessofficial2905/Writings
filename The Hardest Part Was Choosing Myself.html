The Hardest Part Was
Choosing Myself,

But I Did.

Words by Princess

Read
There comes a moment, the quiet kind, the sacred kind,
when you finally look at yourself and realize you are no longer the person you once were.

There comes a moment, the quiet kind, the sacred kind, when you finally look at yourself and realize you are no longer the person who begged for clarity, watered down your worth, clung to potential, or waited for someone who kept choosing everything but you. Letting go didn't happen in a single moment. It happened slowly, painfully, beautifully. It happened in the tiny decisions you made every day: every time you chose peace over chaos, truth over hope, boundaries over attachment. And without noticing it, you became someone entirely new, someone stronger, wiser, softer in the right places and firmer in the ones that once broke too easily.

Letting go wasn't about losing them.

It was about finding you.

The you who stopped apologizing for feeling deeply.

The you who no longer settled for half-love.

The you who learned that silence isn't peace, and confusion isn't love.

The you who realized that your heart is a gift, not something to be taken for granted.

The you who finally understood that some people are not meant to stay, they are meant to show you what you should never again accept.

There is a version of you now who breathes easier.

Who doesn't carry the weight of someone else's inconsistency.

Who doesn't question whether she is enough.

Who doesn't walk on emotional eggshells.

Who doesn't shrink herself for connection.

And that version of you exists because you let go of what hurt you, even when you loved it.

Letting go was the hardest thing you ever did, My friend, but it was also the most defining.

God watched every step of your becoming. He saw the nights you cried but still chose to move forward in the morning. He saw the heartbreak that cracked you open just enough for Him to pour healing into the spaces you once tried to fill with people. He saw the courage it took to walk away from what felt familiar, even when it wasn't healthy. And He guided you, gently, repeatedly, toward the woman you were always meant to become. Not bitter, not hardened, not guarded… but grounded, whole, and anchored in your worth.

One day soon, you will look at this version of yourself and whisper, "I'm proud of you." Because you didn't just survive, you transformed. You didn't just let go, you rose. You didn't just lose someone, you found the woman God was shaping beneath the pain. And the life ahead of you will not mirror the heartbreak behind you. The love that's coming will not resemble the love you walked away from. The peace that fills your days will be the peace you once prayed for with trembling hands.

Letting go didn't break you.

Letting go rebuilt you.

And the person you became,

soft, strong, certain, aligned,

is the proof that endings are not failures…

they are doorways to your becoming.
