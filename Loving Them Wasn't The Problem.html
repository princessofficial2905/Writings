An Essay on Love & Letting Go

Loving Them Wasn't
the Problem.

Waiting for Them Was.

Princess February 2026 3 min read
Read

There comes a moment when you finally admit to yourself that the thing that kept you holding on wasn't the reality of the relationship, it was the hope of what it could be. Hope is powerful. Hope is beautiful. But sometimes hope becomes the very thing that blinds you to the truth right in front of you. You weren't holding on to their effort because their effort was inconsistent. You weren't holding on to their words because their words rarely became actions. You were holding on to the potential, the possibility, the dream of the love you wished they were capable of giving. And when your heart loves deeply, it clings to hope even when logic is gone.

✦

You told yourself things would eventually change.

You believed they just needed time.

You believed they would finally choose you wholeheartedly.

You believed the version of them you saw in rare, tender moments could become permanent.

But hope, when placed in the wrong hands, becomes exhaustion.

It becomes waiting.

It becomes self-sacrifice in disguise.

It becomes a belief that maybe tomorrow will be different, even when every yesterday looked exactly the same.

But God saw how much you were carrying alone. He saw the nights you prayed for clarity and still woke up choosing hope instead of peace. He saw the effort you were pouring into something that was giving so little back. He saw your loyalty being stretched into self-neglect. And with all the love in His heart, He began gently loosening your attachment, not because He wanted to take something good away, but because He wanted to protect you from a future that would have drained you even more.

The hope you had was never wrong. It was simply misplaced.

One day, My friend, you will look back and realize that the hope you had was never wrong, it was simply misplaced. You were meant to hope for love, yes, but not that version of love. Not a love you had to fight alone for. Not a love you had to earn. Not a love you had to wait endlessly for. The right love won't require you to hold on until you're exhausted. It won't require you to convince someone of your worth. It won't require you to wait for "one day." It will show up, present, steady, certain.

And in that moment, you'll understand that hope was never the problem. Hope was the gift. Hope was the light. Hope was the reminder that you still believed in love even after being disappointed by it. And that same hope will lead you to someone who matches your effort, meets your heart, and shows you why the past had to end.

✦

You didn't hold on too long because you were weak,

you held on because you loved honestly.

And now, God is guiding you toward a love

where you won't have to hold on alone.
