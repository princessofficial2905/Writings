The Parts of Me I Lost Loving You.

You don't notice it at first. The losing happens slowly.

Written by Princess

finding your way back

· · ·

There comes a moment, usually in the quiet after everything has fallen apart, when you finally see how much of yourself you gave away while trying to love someone who didn't know how to love you back.

You don't notice it at first, the losing happens slowly. A little compromise here, a silenced emotion there, a swallowed truth, a dimmed light, a softened boundary. You start bending so gently you don't even feel yourself breaking. You shape yourself around their comfort, their moods, their inconsistencies, their needs, until one day you wake up and whisper, "Where did I go?" Loving someone should never cost you your identity, but sometimes, in the effort to keep them close, you slowly drift away from the person you were meant to be.

And the saddest part is that you didn't lose yourself out of weakness, you lost yourself out of love. You cared deeply. You believed in the connection. You saw their pain and tried to heal it. You saw their potential and tried to nurture it. You saw the goodness in them and ignored the ways they kept wounding you. You told yourself that love means patience, forgiveness, understanding, and while those things are true, you forgot the most important part: love should never require you to disappear. But you kept giving, hoping that your sacrifice would eventually create the relationship you dreamed of. Instead, it slowly erased the parts of your heart that needed protecting the most.

But here is the truth God wants you to feel gently:

What you lost in the wrong love, He will restore in the right one.

God saw the pieces of yourself you laid at their feet.

He saw the dreams you delayed, the boundaries you ignored, the emotions you buried, the strength you silenced.

He saw how hard you tried to make a home out of someone who never made room for your heart.

And now, He is guiding you back to yourself, one healed piece at a time.

This loss you feel isn't the end of who you are; it's the beginning of who you're becoming.

One day, you will look in the mirror and see the return of the softness you thought you lost.

You will hear your own voice again, strong, steady, certain.

You will feel your worth settling back into your bones.

You will reclaim the parts of yourself you handed away: your joy, your confidence, your strength, your boundaries, your peace.

And with each piece that returns, you will understand that losing yourself wasn't your failure, it was your awakening.

It will honor you.fully
It will grow with you.deeply
It will love you without asking you to disappear.wholly

My friend, you didn't lose yourself forever. You are simply finding your way back home, to the version of you that God always intended you to be.
