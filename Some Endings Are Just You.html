01  /  The Pieces We Lose

There comes
a moment

There comes a moment in your healing when you look back at the love you tried so hard to hold onto and finally see the truth you couldn't see back then, you didn't just lose a person, you lost pieces of yourself trying to keep them. You lost your peace trying to understand their silence. You lost your confidence trying to earn their attention. You lost your sleep over someone who didn't lose a moment over you. You lost your voice while trying not to upset them. You lost your joy trying to decode mixed signals. And the saddest part is that you didn't even realize you were losing these pieces, because you were too busy trying not to lose them.

You convinced yourself that if you loved them harder, they would love you back.

If you understood them more, they would finally understand you.

If you stayed loyal, they would eventually stay consistent.

But love doesn't work like that.

Love is not supposed to cost you the parts of yourself that make you who you are.

And any love that demands your emotional sacrifice, your self-worth, your identity, or your peace…

is not love, it's self-destruction disguised as devotion.

02  /  What God Saw

But God saw
everything

There were moments you stayed even when your heart whispered, "You're hurting yourself." Moments you bent until you broke, moments you lowered your standards even though your soul knew better, moments you accepted treatment far below what your heart deserved. But you stayed because you loved honestly. You stayed because you saw potential. You stayed because you hoped they would rise to meet you. And that hope, as beautiful and sincere as it was, became the very thing that kept you tied to a love that was slowly draining you.

But God saw everything you lost. He saw the tears you cried to protect someone who wouldn't protect you. He saw the pieces of yourself scattered every time you tried to make things work. He saw the way your spirit dimmed trying to brighten someone else's. And He knew you deserved more, far more, than a love that only grew when it was convenient and faded when it required effort. So He began removing them, softening your attachment, nudging you gently toward a truth you weren't ready to accept.

03  /  Coming Home

One day,
My friend

One day, My friend, you will look back and realize that everything you lost was not taken, it was returned to you. Your peace will return. Your confidence will return. Your voice will return. Your joy will return. Your worth will feel like yours again. And when the right love enters your life, a love that doesn't drain you, doesn't confuse you, doesn't make you shrink, you will understand why you had to lose the wrong person to find the right version of yourself.

Key Passage

You cannot lose yourself trying to keep someone who was never meant to stay.

Closing

You didn't lose them.
You lost pieces of yourself trying to keep them.

And now, God is bringing you into a chapter where you finally get those pieces back, whole, healed, and glowing with a love that feels like coming home.
