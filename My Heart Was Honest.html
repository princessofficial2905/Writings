A confession in words

My Heart Was Honest.

Their Actions Weren't.

Princess  •  February 2026
Read

There comes a moment in your healing when you finally understand that the trust you offered so freely wasn't a mistake, it was a reflection of your purity. You trusted because your heart was honest. You trusted because you believed people meant what they said. You trusted because you assumed others loved with the same sincerity you carried naturally. But somewhere along the way, that trust was mishandled, taken advantage of, or met with inconsistency. And when trust is broken, it's not just the connection that gets wounded, it's the part of you that once believed goodness always comes back in equal measure.

You didn't give your trust blindly.

You gave it because you wanted to believe in someone.

You gave it because you thought love and loyalty were universal languages.

You gave it because your heart wasn't built for half-truths, mixed signals, or masks.

But not everyone values trust the same way.

Some people take it as permission to take more.

Some see it as an opportunity to disappoint without accountability.

Some hold it carelessly because they never learned how sacred it is.

✦ ✦ ✦

And still, My friend, you kept trusting.

Not because you ignored the signs,

but because you hoped their actions would eventually rise to match the trust you offered.

You hoped they would appreciate the chance you gave them.

You hoped they would hold your heart gently, because that's how you held theirs.

But hope cannot replace character, and trust cannot build a foundation when only one person is protecting it.

God saw how much trust you placed in people who didn't guard it well. He saw the disappointment you tried to swallow. He saw the doubt that grew in places where certainty should have lived. He saw you questioning yourself, wondering if you were wrong to trust so easily.

"Your trust was not the problem. You just gave it to the wrong hands."

A quiet whisper into your heart

One day, My friend, you will meet someone who treats your trust like treasure.

Someone who doesn't play with your vulnerability.

Someone who doesn't take your openness for granted.

Someone whose actions are steady enough to quiet every fear.

Someone who understands that trust is earned through consistency, not words.

And in their presence, you will feel your ability to trust again, not because you forgot the past, but because you finally feel safe in the present.

Your trust was never naïve.

It was a gift,

and soon, it will be received

by someone who knows exactly how to honor it.
