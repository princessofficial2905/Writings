A letter to the one still punishing themselves

It's Time to Forgive Yourself

You've been carrying it for so long, haven't you?

Written by Princess

let it burn down, let it go

The words you said that hurt someone.
The words you didn't say when you should have.
The choice you made that changed everything.
The choice you didn't make that still haunts you.
The questions you replay over and over.
The version of yourself you wish you had been.
let go

You replay it over and over. What if you had done it differently? What if you had been better, smarter, stronger, braver? What if you had just known then what you know now?

You punish yourself with these questions. Every single day. You wake up and the first thought is often about what you did wrong. You go to sleep and it's the last thing on your mind.

You've apologized a thousand times in your head. You've imagined going back and fixing it. You've beaten yourself up more than anyone else ever could.

And here's what I need you to hear:
It's time to forgive yourself.

I know you don't think you deserve it. I know you think what you did was too bad, too wrong, too hurtful. I know you think that if you forgive yourself, it means what you did was okay.

But that's not what forgiveness means.

Forgiving yourself doesn't mean what you did was right. It doesn't erase the hurt. It doesn't make the mistake disappear.

Forgiving yourself means you finally stop drowning in something that already happened. Something you can't change. Something you've already paid for a thousand times over in your own heart.

You were doing the best you could with what you knew at the time.

With the pain you were carrying.

With the wounds you hadn't healed yet.

With the tools you had.

Was it enough? Maybe not. Did you mess up? Yes. Did you hurt someone? Probably. Did you hurt yourself? Definitely.

But you're human. And humans mess up. They make wrong choices. They say things they regret. They hurt people they love. They fail. They fall. They break.

And then, if they're lucky, they learn. They grow. They become better.

You're not the same person who made that mistake.
You've grown since then. You've learned. You've changed.

The fact that you feel this guilt, this remorse, that proves you're not the same person anymore. The person you were made that mistake. The person you are now carries the lesson. But you don't have to carry the punishment forever.

If someone you loved came to you with the same guilt you're carrying, what would you tell them?

You'd hug them. You'd tell them it's okay.

You'd tell them everyone makes mistakes.

You'd tell them to learn from it and move forward.

You'd tell them they deserve to heal.

So why can't you give yourself that same compassion?

You've already learned from your mistake. You've already grown. You've already become better. Continuing to punish yourself doesn't help anyone. Not the person you hurt. Not the world. And definitely not you.

Holding onto this guilt doesn't make you a good person. It just makes you a person in pain.

And you've been in pain long enough.

01

Your peace matters. Your healing matters. Your ability to move forward and be better matters.

02

You can choose to stop letting that mistake define you. You can choose to be more than the worst thing you've ever done.

03

You can choose to forgive yourself and use what you learned to be better from this day forward.

I know it's scary. You've lived with this guilt for so long that it almost feels comfortable. Familiar. Like an old friend you don't know how to live without.

But imagine what your life could be like without it. Imagine waking up and not immediately feeling that heavy weight on your chest. Imagine going through your day without that voice in your head constantly reminding you of what you did wrong.

Imagine being free.

Not all at once. Forgiveness is a process, not a moment. Some days will be easier than others. Some days that guilt will try to come back and remind you of what happened. But each day, choose again. Choose to be gentle with yourself. Choose to remember that you're doing better now. Choose to believe that you deserve peace.

Say it out loud if you need to: "I forgive myself."

It might feel strange. It might feel wrong. It might feel like a lie at first. But keep saying it. Keep choosing it. Keep believing it. Because it's true.

You deserve forgiveness.
From others, yes.
But most importantly, from yourself.

You are worthy. You are more than your mistakes.

You've carried this weight long enough. You've punished yourself enough. You've suffered enough.

It's time to put it down.
It's time to heal.
It's time to be free.

Forgive yourself. Not because what you did was okay. But because you are okay. You are worthy. You are more than your mistakes. And you deserve to live without this weight crushing you.

Let it go. Please. For your own sake.

You've been forgiven by the person who matters most.

Yourself.
Now believe it.

With compassion and understanding,

Someone who knows how hard forgiveness can be
