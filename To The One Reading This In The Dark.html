To the One Reading This
in the Dark

By Princess  ·  February 2026  ·  3 min read
I see you.

It's late, isn't it? Everyone else is asleep. The world is quiet. And here you are, alone with your thoughts, scrolling through your phone because sleep won't come. Or maybe it will come, but you're afraid of what you'll dream about.

You're reading this in the dark, not just the darkness of your room, but the darkness you've been carrying inside. The kind nobody sees. The kind you hide behind smiles and "I'm fine" and "I'm just tired."

I want you to know something: you're not alone in this darkness.

I know it feels like it. I know it feels like everyone else has their life figured out while you're barely holding yours together. I know it feels like you're the only one awake at 2 AM with a heart full of worries and a mind that won't stop racing.

But you're not.

Right now, somewhere in the world, someone else is reading these words feeling exactly what you're feeling. Someone else is crying quietly so nobody hears. Someone else is wondering if tomorrow will be easier or just another day to survive.

You're not weak for feeling this way. You're not broken. You're not too much or not enough.

You're human. And being human means sometimes the weight gets heavy. Sometimes the light feels far away. Sometimes you don't recognize yourself anymore.

I don't know what brought you to this moment. Maybe it was a loss that shattered you. Maybe it was words that cut deeper than any wound. Maybe it was a dream that died, a person who left, or a version of yourself you can't find anymore.

Maybe it's nothing you can name. Maybe it's just this heavy, tired feeling that's been following you around, and you don't know how to shake it off.

Whatever it is, I need you to hear this: You are still here. And that matters.

I know you don't feel strong right now. I know you feel like you're barely making it. But do you know what strength really is? It's not about being unbreakable. It's about breaking and still showing up the next day.

It's reading these words at 3 AM when you could have given up hours ago.
It's getting out of bed when staying there feels safer.
It's surviving another day when your heart feels too tired to beat.

That's you. That's what you're doing. Even in the dark, especially in the dark, you're still here.

And I know you're tired of being told "it gets better." Because right now, in this moment, better feels impossible. It feels like a lie people tell to make themselves feel helpful.

But here's what I can promise you: this moment will pass. Not the pain, maybe. Not the sadness. But this exact moment, this heaviness, this loneliness, this 3 AM feeling, it will pass.

The sun will come up. It always does. Not because you asked it to, not because you deserve it, but because that's what the sun does. It rises. Again and again. Even after the darkest nights.

And so will you.

You've done it before. Every single dark night you've ever had, you've survived. Every moment you thought would break you, you're still here after. You might be tired. You might be different. But you're still here.

So tonight, right now, be gentle with yourself. Stop punishing yourself for not being okay. Stop comparing your darkness to someone else's light. Stop thinking you should be further along, happier, better, fixed.

You're exactly where you need to be. Even if where you are hurts.

Put your phone down soon. Close your eyes. Breathe slowly. And know that tomorrow, when you wake up, you don't have to be perfect. You don't have to be happy. You just have to be.

And that will be enough.

You will be enough. You always have been. Even in the dark. Especially in the dark.

I see you. And you're going to be okay.

Maybe not tonight. Maybe not tomorrow.

But you will be.

Keep going, dear one. The world needs your light, even when you can't see it yourself.

With hope for your tomorrow,

Someone who's been in the dark too
