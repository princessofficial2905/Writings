A Personal Essay

The Strength I Found

in Falling Apart

By Princess  •  February 2026

Read

There is a version of strength the world never talks about, the kind you discover not when you are holding everything together, but when your entire life feels like it is falling apart in your hands. You didn't feel strong in those moments. You felt overwhelmed, exhausted, confused, broken in places you didn't even know could break. You cried in silence because you didn't want to burden anyone. You showed up for the world while your heart was cracking behind the scenes. You kept breathing when every part of you wanted to collapse. And even though you didn't recognize it then, that was strength. Not the loud, heroic kind, but the raw, human kind. The kind that comes from surviving pain you weren't prepared for and still choosing to keep going.

Falling apart was never a sign of weakness. It was the moment your soul finally released everything it had been carrying alone. The moment you realized you couldn't pretend anymore. The moment the mask slipped, the façade broke, and the truth poured out. And even though it felt like the worst moment of your life, it was also the beginning of something sacred. You were making space, space for healing, space for clarity, space for God to work in areas you'd been trying to manage on your own. You thought you were losing control, but in reality, you were being rebuilt from the inside out.

✦   ✦   ✦
And God was there through all of it. Not judging. Not disappointed. Not distant. He was closest to you in the moments when you felt the most broken. He held your heart when you thought it would shatter completely. He steadied your spirit when your knees gave out. He listened to every wordless prayer, every exhausted breath, every tear that fell without anyone noticing. Falling apart didn't push Him away, it pulled Him closer. It gave Him access to parts of you you'd been holding too tightly, parts He needed to heal so you could step into the life He has prepared for you.
✦
And slowly, quietly, you began to rise. Your strength didn't come from force; it came from surrender. It came from admitting you couldn't carry everything alone. It came from learning to trust the process instead of controlling the outcome. It came from realizing that even broken pieces can be the beginning of something beautiful when placed in the hands of the One who made you.
✦   ✦   ✦

One day, My friend, you will look back at the version of yourself who fell apart and feel nothing but love and gratitude.

She wasn't weak, she was brave enough to break. Brave enough to feel. Brave enough to collapse so she could rebuild on foundations that were real, steady, and healthy. She is the reason you are stronger now. She is the reason you found peace. She is the reason you're still standing.

✦
The strength you have today wasn't born from perfection, it was born from survival, surrender, and the God who held you together while everything else fell apart.
