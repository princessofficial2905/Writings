The Emotions
I Was Never Allowed to Feel.

There comes a moment in your healing when you finally acknowledge that for most of your life, you were not allowed to feel everything you felt. You were told to be strong when you were exhausted. You were told to stay quiet when your heart was breaking. You were told to forgive instantly when you were still bleeding inside. You were told to "let it go," "move on," "stop being sensitive," "don't make it a big deal." Somewhere along the line, you learned to bury your emotions instead of expressing them.

You learned to swallow your sadness, hide your anger, silence your needs, and numb your disappointments. You became the person who held everything in because it never felt safe to let anything out. And now, as an adult, you carry wounds that were never given permission to surface.

It was not your fault. You were raised to believe that emotions made you weak. That crying made you dramatic. That feeling deeply made you difficult. You learned to apologize for having a heart. You learned to shrink your voice to keep the peace. You learned to pretend you were fine because no one knew how to hold the version of you that wasn't. And without realizing it, you became emotionally self-sufficient, carrying your pain alone because you did not want to be a burden to anyone.

The truth you have finally grown brave enough to face

Your emotions were never the problem, the lack of space for them was. You were not wrong for feeling intensely. You were not wrong for needing reassurance. You were not wrong for asking for clarity. You were not wrong for wanting to be understood. You were not wrong for being human. The world simply taught you the wrong lessons about your heart.

And God saw every emotion you were forced to bury.
He saw the moments you cried quietly so no one would hear.
He saw the times you swallowed your truth
because you did not want to upset anyone.

"I never asked you to be this strong alone."

He saw the shame you felt for emotions that were completely valid. God created you with emotions on purpose, not as weaknesses, but as signals, as truth-bearers, as guides. He gave you a sensitive heart because sensitivity is a gift, not a flaw.

One day, my friend, you will be surrounded by people who make space for your emotions instead of silencing them. People who do not call you "too much" because they finally understand the depth of your heart. People who do not make you feel ashamed for needing comfort or clarity. People who listen. People who hold. People who stay. And when that love arrives, you will realize that there was never anything wrong with your emotions, you just spent years in environments that could not hold the weight of your soul.
