The
Things
Your Best Friend Never Told You
8 min read March 2026 Princess
Scroll

They loved you. Genuinely loved you. But sometimes loving you also meant swallowing the bitter taste of comparison.

I. The Silence of Comparison

They never told you they were jealous.

Not of your life exactly. But of parts of it. The parts that came easier to you. The relationship you had that they wanted. The confidence that seemed natural to you but impossible for them. The way people were drawn to you.

They loved you. Genuinely loved you. But sometimes loving you also meant swallowing the bitter taste of comparison. And they never told you because they didn't want you to think their love wasn't real.

II. Words That Landed Differently

They never told you how much your words hurt sometimes. When you were joking around and said something that hit a wound you didn't know was there. When you gave advice that felt like judgment. When you talked about your problems and didn't realize your problems were their dreams.

They laughed it off. They said "I know, right?" They pretended it was fine. Because best friends don't make everything about them. Because they didn't want to be sensitive. Because they were supposed to be happy for you, not hurt by you.

III. Grieving What Was Slipping

They never told you they felt like they were losing you. When you got into that relationship. When you got that new job. When you moved. When your life changed in ways that naturally created distance.

They supported you. They celebrated with you. They said all the right things. But inside they were grieving. Grieving the version of your friendship that was slipping away. Terrified that they'd become less important to you. That they'd be replaced. That you'd outgrow them.

They never told you they needed you more than you needed them. That you were their person in ways they weren't sure they were yours.

IV. Always Second

They never told you they felt like they were always second place. Second to your partner. Second to your other friends. Second to your family. Second to everyone. Like they only mattered to you when no one better was available.

They never told you how much it hurt when you canceled plans. When you forgot important things. When you didn't text back. When you were "too busy." When they could see you making time for everyone else but not for them.

They said "no worries" and "we'll reschedule" and "I totally understand." But they didn't understand. They were hurt. They were starting to wonder if the friendship was one-sided. If you even really cared.

V. Alone With It

They never told you about the times they needed you and you weren't there. Not because you were cruel. Just because you didn't know. Because they didn't tell you. Because they didn't want to be needy. Because they were afraid you'd think they were too much.

So they suffered alone. Through heartbreak, through crisis, through the hardest moments of their life. And they smiled and said they were fine and you believed them because it was easier than seeing they weren't.

VI. Every Single Time

They never told you they noticed when you pulled away. When your texts got shorter. When your calls got less frequent. When you stopped making plans. When you stopped trying.

They noticed every single time. They felt every decrease in effort. They calculated every day that passed without hearing from you. And they never said anything because they didn't want to seem clingy. Because they were hoping you'd come back on your own.

VII. The Anger They Swallowed

They never told you they were angry at you. For not noticing their pain. For not asking deeper questions. For not seeing past their "I'm fine." For being so wrapped up in your own life that you missed theirs falling apart.

They swallowed that anger. They told themselves you were busy. They made excuses for you. They protected you from their hurt because that's what friends do. Even when it was eating them alive.

They never told you about the friendship breakups they've been through before. About the best friend who left. About why they're so careful now. About why they sometimes push you away before you can leave.

VIII. The Fear of Being Left

They never told you how scared they are that you'll do the same thing. That one day you'll realize you don't need them. That you'll find better friends. That you'll move on and leave them behind like everyone else did.

They never told you how much your friendship means to them. How you're the only person who really knows them. How you're the one they think of first with good news or bad. How you're the person who makes them feel less alone in the world.

They assume you know. But they've never actually said it. Because saying it feels vulnerable. Because what if you don't feel the same way? What if their most important relationship is just casual to you?

IX. What Tomorrow Might Take

They never told you they're worried about the future. Worried that life will pull you in different directions. Worried that marriage or kids or careers will make your friendship a lower priority. Worried that "best friends forever" was just something you say, not something that actually happens.

They never told you about the times they defended you. When others talked badly about you. When people questioned your choices. When someone tried to turn them against you. They had your back without you ever knowing you needed defending.

X. The Admiration Left Unsaid

They never told you how much they admire you. How they see your strength even when you don't. How they're inspired by you. How they want to be more like you in ways they'd never admit out loud.

They never told you they're proud of you. For how far you've come. For what you've overcome. For who you're becoming. They think it but they don't say it because it feels awkward or they assume you know or they don't want to make it weird.

XI. The Apologies They Held Back

They never told you they're sorry. For the times they let you down. For the times they weren't the friend you deserved. For the times they were dealing with their own stuff and couldn't be there for you. For the ways they've failed you even though they never meant to.

They never told you they're scared of growing apart. That they've already noticed it happening. That they can feel the friendship shifting. That they don't know how to stop it or if it can even be stopped.

They never told you they miss you. Miss how things used to be. Miss the closeness you had before life got complicated. Miss feeling like your person instead of just one of your people.

XII. The Words That Stayed

They never told you about the times they almost said something. Almost told you they were hurt. Almost asked if you still cared. Almost admitted they needed more from you. But they stopped themselves. Because they didn't want to rock the boat. Because they were afraid of the answer. Because they didn't want to lose you by asking for too much.

They never told you they're trying. Trying to be understanding. Trying to not take things personally. Trying to give you space while also wanting you closer. Trying to be the best friend you deserve even when they're not sure they're getting the same in return.

They never told you they're breaking. Under the weight of unsaid words. Under the fear of losing you. Under the loneliness of loving someone who might not love them back the same way.

XIII. Why They Stay Quiet

They never told you because they're scared. Scared of being too much. Scared of pushing you away. Scared of finding out you don't value the friendship the way they do. Scared that speaking up will be the thing that ends it.

So they stay quiet. They swallow their hurt. They accept whatever you give them. They make excuses for you. They convince themselves it's enough.

But maybe it's not enough. Maybe they're drowning in unsaid words. Maybe the friendship is slowly dying from all the things that never get said.

XIV. Or Maybe It's You

Or maybe you're reading this and recognizing yourself. Maybe you're the best friend who never told them. Maybe you have your own list of unspoken truths. Maybe you're both carrying silence that's creating distance.

If your best friend is still in your life, maybe it's time to have the conversation. To say the hard things. To be vulnerable. To stop assuming and start asking. To stop protecting and start being real.

"Do you feel as close to me as I feel to you?"
"Have I hurt you without knowing it?"
"Are we okay?"
"Do you still want this friendship as much as I do?"

Scary questions. Vulnerable questions. Questions that might hurt. But questions that might also save the friendship before it's too late.

Or maybe it is too late. Maybe they're already gone. Maybe the friendship already ended. Maybe the distance already won. Maybe the things they never told you will stay forever unsaid.

And if that's the case, you can grieve it. You can wish you'd known. You can wish you'd asked. You can forgive yourself for not seeing what they never showed you.

XV. What We Carry

Best friends aren't mind readers. Sometimes we miss things. Sometimes we're so caught up in our own lives that we don't see someone else's pain. Sometimes we fail people we love even when we never meant to.

The things your best friend never told you? Maybe you'll never hear them. Maybe the friendship will heal without words. Maybe the words will finally be said. Maybe you'll drift apart still holding unspoken truths.

Whatever happens, know this: friendships are complicated. People are complicated. Love is complicated.

We all carry things we never say. Fears we never voice. Hurts we never share. And sometimes those silences create distance we never intended.

Say the things. Ask the questions. Take the risk.

But awareness is the first step. Asking is the first step. Being vulnerable is the first step.

Your best friend might never tell you these things. But you can ask. You can create space for honesty. You can show them it's safe to be real.

And maybe, just maybe, the things they never told you can finally be said. Before it's too late. Before the friendship becomes another relationship that died from silence.

Because the things left unsaid might be the very things that could save it.

Or they might be the things that finally set you both free from a friendship that wasn't working anyway.

Either way, the truth is better than the silence.

✶    ✶    ✶

The things your best friend never told you? They deserve to be heard. Even if it's hard. Even if it hurts. Even if it changes everything.

Because that's what real friendship is. Not perfect. Not silent. Not pretending. Real. Honest. Brave enough to say the hard things.

Before it's too late. While there's still time. While the friendship still matters enough to fight for.

Say the things. Hear the things. Save the friendship. Or let it go with honesty instead of silence.

But don't let the things they never told you be the reason the friendship dies.

Speak. Listen. Be real.

That's what best friends do. Or what they should do.

Before the silence becomes too loud to overcome.
